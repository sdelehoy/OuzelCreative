How to Set a Goal Before a Networking Event (And Actually Enjoy It)

February 9, 2026

Networking events can feel intimidating. Maybe you’re shy. Maybe you don’t know what to say. Or maybe you just don’t see the point.

But here’s the thing: Networking doesn’t have to be awkward. It can be fun, easy, and even rewarding—if you go in with a plan.

The problem? No clear goal = no motivation. You show up, wander around, and leave feeling like you wasted your time.

The solution? Set a simple, fun goal before you go. Something small. Something doable. Something that makes networking feel less like a chore and more like a game.

Here Are 5 Easy Goals to make Your Next Networking Event a Win.

Problem: "I'm Too Nervous to Network"

Solution: Meet 3 new people. You don’t need to talk to everyone. Just 3 people. That’s it.

Why it works: Small numbers feel less scary.

How to do it:

  1. Walk up and say, "Hi, I’m [Name]. What brought you here today?”
  2. Listen. Ask follow-up questions.
  3. If the convo fizzles, smile and move on.

Bonus: If you meet 3 people, you’ve already succeeded!

Problem: "I Don't Know What to Say"

Solution: Learn something new. Your goal? Ask one interesting question and actually listen to the answer.

Why it works: You're not "selling" yourself—you're learning.

How to do it:

  • "What’s the coolest eco-project you’ve worked on?”
  • "What’s one thing you wish more people knew about sustainability?”
  • "What’s your favorite way to reduce waste?"

Bonus: You’ll leave with new ideas (and maybe even a collaboration!).

Problem: "Networking Feels Like a Chore"

Solution: Make it a game. Turn networking into a fun challenge.

Here's how:

  • The "Business Card Bingo" Game – Give yourself points for:
    • Meeting someone in a different industry
    • Finding a shared interest
    • Getting a laugh out of someone
  • The "Ask for Advice" Challenge – Instead of pitching yourself, ask for tips.
    • "How did you get started in [their field]?"

Bonus: Games take the pressure off and make networking feel playful.

Problem: "I Don't Know What I Want From Networking"

Solution: Find one useful connection. Your goal? Find one person who could help you (or vise vesa).

Why it works: Focus > chaos.

How to do it:

  • "I’m looking for [X]. Do you know anyone who might fit?”
  • "What’s the best networking tip you’ve ever gotten?"

Bonus: Even one great connection can lead to big opportunities.

Problem: "I Forget to Follow Up"

Solution: Collect one contact to follow up with. Your goal? Exchange info with one person and send them a message within 24 hours.

Why it works: Follow-ups turn strangers into contacts.

How to do it:

  • "It was great meeting you! Let’s connect on LinkedIn.”
  • "I loved talking about [topic]. Let’s grab coffee soon!"

Bonus: A quick follow-up keeps the conversation going.
