How to Craft an Elevator Pitch That Actually Works

February 9, 2026

Let’s be real—networking events can feel like a speed-dating session for professionals. You meet someone new, they ask, "So, what do you do?" and suddenly your brain short-circuits.

Cue the awkward rambling. Cue the "I help people… um… it’s kind of complicated.”

Enter: The Elevator Pitch.

This isn’t just a fancy term for a 30-second bio. It’s your secret weapon for making a killer first impression. And the best part? You can borrow a foolproof strategy from StoryBrand, a framework created by Donald Miller, to make yours shine.

What's an Elevator Pitch For, Anyway?

To put it briefly, it’s your answer to "What do you do?"—but way more engaging.

A great elevator pitch

  • Is short and clear
  • Grabs attention
  • Makes you memorable
  • Opens doors (hello, new opportunities!)

The key? Structure. And that’s where StoryBrand comes in.

How to Build an Unforgettable Elevator Pitch (StoryBrand Style)

Step 1: Start with the Problem

People don’t care about what you do—they care about why it matters. Most people are looking for products and services that solve a problem they are dealing with. Discussing the problem or pain point that most of your clients face will make your audience lean in and want to hear more.

Example

❌ Don't say, "I’m a sustainability consultant.”

✅ Instead, try saying, “Many businesses who care about the environment struggle to cut waste without sacrificing profits.”

Why it works: When you dial in to your audience, you begin speaking to their pain point—something they actually care about. Their ears perk up and they want to know if you can solve their problem.

Step 2: Share Your Solution

Now, introduce yourself as that person who can solve that problem. Talk about your solution to the problem you just stated.

Example

❌ Don't say, "I work with a green team."

✅ Instead, try saying, "I help small businesses reduce their carbon footprint without breaking the bank. My team makes sustainability simple—so you can focus on growth.”

Why it works: You’re positioning yourself as the answer to their problem. You’ll know when you’ve piqued their interest, when you hear them say, “Tell me more…”

Step 3: Highlight the Result

End with the transformation they’ll experience. Talk about the successful result someone will experience when they buy your product or service.

Example

❌ Don't say, "I run a recycling program."

✅ Instead, try saying, "I help companies turn trash into cash—so they save money and the planet at the same time."

Why it works: People want to know what’s in it for them, and they are looking for someone they trust who can help them win the day.

Putting It All Together

Here’s a full elevator pitch:

Many businesses struggle to cut waste without sacrificing profits. I help small businesses reduce their carbon footprint without breaking the bank. My team makes sustainability simple—so you can focus on growth. You’ll get a greener brand, loyal customers, and real cost savings.

Boom. Now that’s a pitch that gets people’s attention.

Why This Works (And Why You Should Care)

  1. It’s Clear – No jargon, no fluff. Just straight-to-the-point value.
  2. It’s Relatable – You’re speaking to their real struggles, not just your job title.
  3. It’s Memorable – People will actually remember what you do (and want to talk to you about it).
