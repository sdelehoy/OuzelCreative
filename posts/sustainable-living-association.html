About Sustainable Living Association

The Sustainable Living Association is a nonprofit that educates people and communities to make healthy and sustainable choices. They have been an active participant and hub in the community of Fort Collins and Northern Colorado for over 25 years.

The Challenge

Sustainable Living Association website, designed 8 years ago, was outdated and did not reflect their vibrant brand identity. The user experience was cumbersome, limiting discovery and donating. In addition, the website did not match SLA’s values of lowering environmental impact. The website had a high carbon footprint, scoring at the lowest rated level on the Ecograder.com calculator.

The Solution

Ouzel Creative collaborated closely with Sustainable Living Association to craft a fresh, visually compelling website. Key features included:

  • A clean design to reflect the brand’s core values
  • Strategic messaging to increase confidence and alignment with supporting SLA
  • Responsive layout optimized for both desktop and mobile devices
  • Integrated platform and sitemap organization enabling easy user experience
  • Educational blog section for visitor engagement
  • SEO-focused content structure and performance to increase organic search rankings
  • Eco-friendly solutions for the website platform, design, hosting and development

Website Platform

Wordpress

WordPress was selected because it can be hosted on any provider that is committed to using renewable energy. Proprietary platforms lock you into their hosting; even if they are green today, their certification could lapse or their sustainability policies could change. With WordPress, you retain the flexibility to switch to a new green hosting provider whenever needed.

Bricks Builder

On top of WordPress, Bricks Builder was selected for its design capabilities and its ability to generate clean, semantic code, which improves SEO, accessibilty, and reduces file size.

Hosting

Kinsta

Kinsta is a reputable managed WordPress hosting provider and a Green Web Foundation verified green host, meaning its data centers run on renewable energy via Google Cloud Platform.

Customers can select the data center that stores their site files; for SLA’s website, the Google Iowa facility was chosen because of its proximity and because it achieves one of the highest rates of carbon free energy on an hourly basis of among the Google servers in North America according to Google’s 2024 Environmental Report.

Kinsta also partners with Cloudflare—another verified green host—for its CDN (Content Delivery Network), which caches files on servers closest to visitors, speeding up load times while reducing the distance data travels and the associated energy use.

Development

Images

During development, Ouzel Creative converted all images to AVIF to shrink file sizes. Icons were optimized and served directly from the site instead of pulling them from an external library, which can delay rendering. Additionally, a plugin was installed to automatically optimize images at upload.

Fonts

The headline font is hosted locally instead of pulling it from an external font library. The body copy uses a system font stack, which relies on the visitor’s own operating-system fonts, eliminating the need to down load an extra font file. Each kilobyte that isn’t transferred translates into saved energy.
