How to Make Connections that Last (And Actually Help People)

February 12, 2026

Let’s be real—networking can feel like a transactional game. You meet someone, exchange business cards, and then… crickets. But what if I told you there’s a better way? A way to build real relationships that last?

The secret? Adding value.

In a world that often feels greedy and self-serving, being the person who helps others is a superpower. And guess what? People remember—and reciprocate—those who genuinely support them.

So, how do you add value in networking? Let’s break it down.

The Problem: Networking Feels Like a One-Way Street

Most people approach networking with "What can you do for me?" on their minds. But here’s the truth: People are more likely to work with those who help them solve problems. If you’re only thinking about what you can get, you’re missing out on what you can give.

The Solution: Be the Person Who Adds Value

Ask, "How can I support you?"

Instead of pitching yourself, ask how you can help.

Example: "I’d love to support your work—what’s one thing you’re focusing on right now?"

Why it works: It shows you care and opens the door for collaboration.

Follow or Connect on Social Media

If you meet someone interesting, follow them on LinkedIn or Instagram.

Example: "I’d love to stay in touch—let’s connect on LinkedIn!"

Why it works: It keeps the conversation going and shows you’re invested.

Make a Referral

Know someone who’d be a great fit for their project? Introduce them!

Example: "I know someone working on [topic]—would you like an intro?"

Why it works: Referrals build trust and strengthen relationships.

Share Useful Information

See an article, tool, or resource that could help them? Send it over!

Example: "I came across this sustainability report—thought you’d find it helpful!"

Why it works: You’re positioning yourself as a go-to resource.

Offer a Unique Perspective

If they’re struggling with a problem, share your insights.

Example: "I faced something similar—here’s what worked for me."

Why it works: You’re not just talking—you’re solving problems.

The Bonus: You Become a Super Connector

When you focus on adding value, something magical happens: People remember you. Opportunities come back to you. You build a stronger network.

And stronger networks? They build stronger communities.

Final Thought: Networking Isn't About Taking—It's About Giving

The next time you’re at a networking event, ask yourself: "How can I help this person?” Shift your mindset from "What can I get?" to "What can I give?" and watch your network grow in ways you never expected.
