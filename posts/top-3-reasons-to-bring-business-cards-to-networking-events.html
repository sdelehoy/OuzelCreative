Top 3 Reasons to Bring Business Cards to a Networking Event

December 24, 2025

If you've ever rolled your eyes at the idea of business cards thinking they're a waste of paper, you're not alone. In an age where we can just tap phones or scan QR codes, why bother with a tiny piece of paper? Well, hold onto your reusable coffee cup because we're about to drop some truth bombs on why physical business cards still slay at networking events.

Have you thought to yourself that business cards are a waste of paper? Spoiler alert: They don’t have to be! And even if they may seem outdated, the benefits might just change your mind. Let’s dive in.

The 3 Reasons Business Cards Still Matter in 2026

1. They Make a Lasting First Impression

Sure, you could just hand someone your phone and say, "Add your contact!" But let’s be real—nothing says "I’m professional and prepared" like a sleek, well-designed business card. A physical card is a tangible reminder of you long after the event is over. A tactile card creates an experience that says something about your expertise, and that feeling is attached to something real. It sits on a desk, gets tucked into a wallet, or even becomes a bookmark (we’ve all done it).

Digital Alternatives (QR codes, phone contacts, etc.) vs. Physical Cards

  • QR codes are quick, but they require the other person to have their phone out and be willing to scan it. Not everyone’s comfortable with that.
  • Phone contacts are easy, but they lack personality and are soon lost in a cloud of names. A business card can include a memorable tagline, all your social handles, or even a handwritten note—things that don’t translate to a digital exchange.

PRO TIP: If you’re worried about waste, opt for a minimalist design with recycled paper or even seed paper that can be planted later. More on that in a sec!

2. They Keep the Conversation Flowing

Networking events are all about energy—you don’t want to kill the vibe by fumbling with your phone while someone’s mid-sentence. Worse yet, a tech fail will sap valuable time to make an impression and quickly distract from connection. Handing over a business card is a smooth, low-pressure way to exchange info without interrupting the flow of conversation.

Digital Alternatives (QR codes, phone contacts, etc.) vs. Physical Cards

  • QR codes can feel impersonal, like you’re just handing out a link instead of a real connection.
  • Phone contacts require the other person to stop what they’re doing, type, and confirm—awkward!
  • A business card, on the other hand, is a seamless way to say, "Let’s keep this conversation going!" without derailing the moment or forcing your way into someone’s personal contact list.

3. They're a Subtle Power Move

Here’s the thing: People notice when you’re prepared. Showing up with a business card signals that you take networking seriously,and that you’re someone worth remembering. It’s a small but mighty way to stand out in a sea of LinkedIn profiles.

Digital Alternatives (QR codes, phone contacts, etc.) vs. Physical Cards

  • QR codes can feel gimmicky if not done right.
  • Phone contacts are forgettable—how many times have you added someone’s info, only to never message them?
  • A business card, though? It’s a physical keepsake that lives in someone’s wallet or desk drawer, reminding them of your awesome conversation.

Bonus: How to Choose Eco-Friendly Business Cards

Okay, so you’re convinced—business cards are still a networking must-have. But how do you make them sustainable? Here’s what to look for:

Recycle Electronic Devices—If possible repair electronic devices and continue using them, or pass them on or sell them to someone who can use it. If they are passed their usable life, have them properly recycled by a certified recycler (see resources below). Devices contain toxic materials as well as precious and heavy metals.

Ways Businesses Can Reduce Their Digital Footprint

Switch to Green Hosting—If you own a website, choose a website hosting provider/cloud service that uses renewable energy. (see resources below).

Optimize SEO—By optimizing for search engines on your website it helps people find what they are looking for on the internet quickly. It can reduce the amount of time and web pages during a person's search so that it uses less energy.

Improve User Experience—One of the main goals of good user experience for a website is making the user journey as streamlined as possible. Intuitive navigation and easy to find information that people are searching for reduces energy wasted.

Use a Content Delivery Network (CDN)—A CDN is a network of servers around the globe that allow you to cache your website files from your hosting provider closer to where your users are located. The less distanced data travels the less energy used. An added benefit is that it provides another layer of security.

Reduce Animations—Implementing animations on your website uses computer processing and thereby uses more power.

Consider Vector Images—Vector images, such as SVG format, are generally smaller in file size than equivalent raster images. Assets on your website, such as logos, icons, and illustrations, can be a vector. Vectors also have the advantage of being able to be used in various sizes without pixelation.

Reduce Website Tracking—Cancel analytics tracking on your website if you are not using the collected data.
