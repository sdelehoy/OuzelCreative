The Hidden Cost of Email

We’ve been taught to reduce paper and that it’s eco-friendly to use digital files and send emails instead. Initially that seemed like a good idea, but now it’s apparent that we have replaced one problem for another.

When you hit “send” on a newsletter, a tiny packet of data darts through the internet, lands in a data center, is stored, and later opened on a phone or laptop. When you save emails, files and photos in the “cloud” they get stored in data centers. Each of those steps requires water and electricity. Data centers have to be maintained 24/7 with cooling and electricity that use up valuable resources.

It’s easy to overlook what is happening because an email feels weightless when it’s not stored in plain sight. Yet the reality is startling. With billions of commercial emails sent every day, the cumulative emissions are comparable to the output of a small city. Humans have accumulated an enormous digital footprint. Traditional ESPs (Email Service Providers) rarely disclose the energy cost of a single send, leaving marketers blind to the hidden environmental price of their campaigns.

Much of the world’s electricity still comes from fossil fuels, so every kilowatt‑hour used creates carbon dioxide (CO₂). Now multiply those tiny amounts by billions of commercial emails sent each day. In short, email marketing has a cumulative environmental footprint that is real, measurable, and growing.

How EcoSend Lowers the Impact of Email

  • Renewable-powered servers
    • What it does: All processing, transmission and storage runs on green energy.
    • Why it matters: Cuts the baseline emissions of each send.
  • Efficient Infastructure
    • What it does: Optimised code and routing reduce the number of server cycles per email.
    • Why it matters: Less energy per message = lower carbon.
  • Carbon-offset tree planting
    • What it does: Every campaign funds verified biodiversity and climate projects, with trees planted through partners such as Restor.eco and Treeapp.
    • Why it matters: Offsets any residual emissions and supports reforestation.
  • Real-time carbon reporting
    • What it does: Dashboards display emissions per campaign and total offsets earned, the number of trees planted, and the projects supported.
    • Why it matters: Gives marketers a tangible sustainability KPI to share with stakeholders.
  • EcoSend badge
    • What it does: A visual badge that can be added to newsletters.
    • Why it matters: Raises awareness of digital impact of email and the environment.

These mechanisms together make EcoSend a complete, end‑to‑end solution for sustainable email. The platform’s “eco‑by‑design” approach means that we can focus on creative strategy rather than worrying about hidden environmental costs.
